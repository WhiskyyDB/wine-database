Relational 3NF Architecture

How WineDB separates producer metadata from vintage expressions and enforces strict schema constraints.

🔗

Third-Normal Form (3NF)

Flat spreadsheets repeat producer foundation years and appellation details thousands of times. WineDB normalizes entities into wineries, wines, vintages, blends, and tasting_profiles linked by strict foreign keys.

🏷️

Controlled VIVC Ampelography

Grape varietal names are automatically normalized against VIVC ampelographic registries during ingestion, mapping synonyms (e.g., Shiraz → Syrah, Cannonau → Grenache) to canonical keys.

Verified Data Guarantees

Strict mathematical check constraints, primary audit registries, and valuation rules enforced at the database layer.

⚖️

Blend Sum Check Trigger

Database trigger trg_check_blend_sum_insert fires on every INSERT or UPDATE to blends. If the cumulative sum across varieties for a vintage exceeds 100.001%, SQLite raises an immediate ABORT.

📊

Valuation Rule (≥ 3 Observations)

Secondary market indexes are calculated strictly from public auction hammer prices. If fewer than 3 independent lots are observed within 18 months, the index remains NULL to prevent speculative bias.

🛡️

Primary Source Audit Registry

Every single cuvee and vintage record links directly to our master data_sources table with exact URLs pointing to US TTB COLA label filings, SAQ/LCBO laboratory sheets, and estate technical notes.

🧪

Quantitative Laboratory Chemistry

Instead of subjective sommelier descriptors alone, WineDB records precise laboratory chemistry: exact residual_sugar_g_l, titratable_acidity_g_l, ph_level, and bottle format volume dimensions (`750ml`, `1500ml`).
